186 def get_compiled_circuits(

188 ) -> List[Circuit]: 

189 """Compile a sequence of circuits with :py:meth:`default_compilation_pass` 

190 and return the list of compiled circuits (does not act in place). 

191 

192 As well as applying a degree of optimisation (controlled by the 

193 `optimisation_level` parameter), this method tries to ensure that the circuits 

194 can be run on the backend (i.e. successfully passed to 

195 :py:meth:`process_circuits`), for example by rebasing to the supported gate set, 

196 or routing to match the connectivity of the device. However, this is not always 

197 possible, for example if the circuit contains classical operations that are not 

198 supported by the backend. You may use :py:meth:`valid_circuit` to check whether 

199 the circuit meets the backend's requirements after compilation. This validity 

200 check is included in :py:meth:`process_circuits` by default, before any circuits 

201 are submitted to the backend. 

202

204 by iterating through the predicates in the `required_predicates` property of the

206 circuit.
