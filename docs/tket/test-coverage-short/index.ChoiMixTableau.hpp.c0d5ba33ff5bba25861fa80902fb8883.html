22 /**
23 * Represents the stabiliser group for a Clifford process with qubit
24 * initialisations/post-selections and mixed initialisations/discards.
25 *
26 * Based on the mixed stabiliser tableau representation of Audenaert & Pleino
27 * 2005 (doi:10.1088/1367-2630/7/1/170), commonly used for representing a
28 * stabilizer code in QEC. We make use of the Choi-Jamiolkovski isomorphism to
29 * represent processes with inputs via the mixed tableau of their Choi state.
30 *
31 * Rows correspond to generators for the stabilizers of the mixed process,
32 * i.e. Pauli measurements with expectation value 1. We will refer to these as
33 * "coherent stabilizers" or "coherent subgroup". With mixed states, there may
34 * exist additional Pauli operators which leave the state unchanged but do
35 * have expectation value 0, which we will refer to as "decoherent
36 * stabilizers" (i.e. the logical operators of the corresponding stabiliser
37 * code). The extra generators for these are not stored since they can be
38 * derived through commutation with the coherent subgroup. Similarly,
39 * destabilisers (detectable errors) can be derived. A future implementation
40 * may wish to always store the decoherent stabilizers and destabilisers for
41 * faster updates.
42 *
43 * Each row is divided into its input segment and output segment. Under the CJ
44 * isomorphism, a row RxS means (in matrix multiplication order) SCR^T = C.
45 * When mapped to a sparse readable representation, independent
46 * SpPauliStabiliser objects are used for each segment, so we no longer expect
47 * their individual phases to be +-1, instead only requiring this on their
48 * product.
49 *
50 * Columns of the tableau are indexed by pair of Qubit id and a tag to
51 * distinguish input vs output. Rows are not maintained in any particular
52 * order, though the `gaussian_form()` method will bring it into row-reduced
53 * echelon form.
54 */
