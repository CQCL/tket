25 /** This contains functions for intersecting sets, and related things.
26 * By interleaving iterators and using lower_bound,
27 * the intersection of sets and similar tasks can often be quite a bit faster
28 * than the obvious method of just going through
29 * the smaller container one-by-one and checking against the larger container.
30 *
31 * In the worst case, these algorithms use O(1) space and time
32 * O([min size].log [min size].log [max size]),
33 * and so asymptotically are no worse than the naive
34 * methods; but in practice they are usually faster.
35 * They're especially good if the intersection is much smaller
36 * than each individual set.
37 *
38 * It is faster with sorted vectors than std::sets
39 * (e.g. std::lower_bound on sorted vectors can take a narrower range,
40 * i.e., it takes a start iterator as well as an end iterator,
